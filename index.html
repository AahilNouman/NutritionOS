Frequently Asked Questions

Evidence-based answers to common nutrition questions.

What is TDEE?

TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the total number of calories your body burns in a 24-hour period. It includes your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) plus the calories you burn through physical activity and digestion.

What is BMR?

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body requires to perform basic life-sustaining functions, such as breathing, circulation, and cell production, while at complete rest.

How many calories should I eat?

To lose weight, you should eat fewer calories than your TDEE (caloric deficit). To gain muscle, you should eat more than your TDEE (caloric surplus). To maintain weight, eat exactly your TDEE. A standard deficit or surplus is usually 10-20% of your TDEE.

How do I lose weight?

Weight loss requires a sustained caloric deficit. Calculate your TDEE, then consume 10-20% fewer calories than you burn each day. Combine this with a high-protein diet and resistance training to preserve muscle mass.

How do I gain muscle?

Muscle gain requires a slight caloric surplus, sufficient protein intake (1.6-2.2g per kg of body weight), and progressive overload through resistance training. Using a bulking calculator helps determine the right surplus to minimize fat gain.

How much protein do I need?

For muscle growth and preservation, aim for 1.6 to 2.2 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight (0.7-1g per pound). Sedentary individuals need less (around 0.8g/kg), but higher protein diets are beneficial for weight management.

What is body recomposition?

Body recomposition is the process of losing body fat and building muscle mass simultaneously. It is best achieved by eating at a slight caloric deficit or maintenance, consuming a high-protein diet, and engaging in intense resistance training.

How accurate is the calculator?

NutritionOS uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, which is considered the gold standard and most accurate formula for estimating BMR in modern populations. However, all calculators provide estimates; you should monitor your progress and adjust intake accordingly.
